Praggnanandhaa Clinches Superbet Chess Classic 2025

Indian Grandmaster R Praggnanandhaa won the Superbet Chess Classic 2025 in Bucharest, defeating top players in thrilling blitz tie-breaks. His strategic brilliance shone through, marking a major milestone for Indian chess.

Read More

At just 19, R Praggnanandhaa outplayed grandmasters like Maxime Vachier-Lagrave and Alireza Firouzja in the blitz tie-breaks to claim the title on May 16, 2025. The tournament, part of the Grand Chess Tour, featured a star-studded lineup, including world champion Gukesh Dommaraju. Praggnanandhaa’s victory came after a intense 73-move battle against Firouzja, showcasing his deep positional understanding and aggressive opening strategies. His win has solidified India’s growing dominance in global chess, with fans celebrating his composure under pressure.

Murali Karthikeyan Shines at Asian Blitz Championship

Indian Grandmaster Murali Karthikeyan secured fourth place at the Asian Individual Blitz Chess Championship in Al Ain, emerging as India’s top performer in the open section.

Read More

Held at the Danat Al Ain Resort Hotel, the Asian Individual Blitz Chess Championship saw fierce competition among Asia’s best players. Murali Karthikeyan’s fourth-place finish on May 11, 2025, highlighted his speed and tactical prowess. The open section was won by 15-year-old Ivan Zemlyanskiia, competing under the FIDE flag, with 8/9 points. Karthikeyan’s performance, alongside Padmini Rout’s fourth place in the women’s section, underscores India’s rising strength in blitz chess. Notably, Indian star Nihal Sarin was absent from the event, surprising fans given his expertise in speed chess.

Chess Banned in Afghanistan Amid Religious Concerns

The Taliban has banned chess in Afghanistan, citing religious concerns, halting a sport that had seen a resurgence in recent years.

Read More

On May 11, 2025, Afghanistan’s interim administration, led by the Taliban, announced an indefinite ban on chess, labeling it a form of gambling under Islamic law. The decision, enforced by the Ministry of Promotion of Virtue and Prevention of Vice, has halted all chess activities, including a planned rapid tournament in a major city. The Afghanistan National Chess Federation, now operating in exile, faces challenges as the sport’s community grapples with the sudden restriction. Local players and cafe owners who hosted informal tournaments expressed disappointment, noting the cultural loss for a game with a decades-long history in the country.

Magnus Carlsen’s Epic Draw Against 143,000 Players

Chess grandmaster Magnus Carlsen faced 143,000 players in a Chess960 match, resulting in a historic draw for Team World.

Read More

In a unique Chess960 (Fischer Random Chess) event on May 21, 2025, Magnus Carlsen took on Team World, comprising 143,000 players from around the globe. The marathon match, played in a randomized chess format, ended in a draw, showcasing the collective strength of the amateur community. Carlsen, known for his dominance, praised the event for boosting chess’s popularity, especially after the surge in interest from the pandemic and media like *The Queen’s Gambit*. The match highlighted the accessibility and strategic depth of Chess960, where piece placement is randomized to emphasize creativity over memorized openings.

Vasyl Ivanchuk’s Stunning Victory at Menorca Open

At 56, Ukrainian GM Vasyl Ivanchuk won the 2025 Menorca Open with an impressive 8/9 score, returning to the world’s top 100.

Read More

Vasyl Ivanchuk, a chess legend, delivered a remarkable performance at the Menorca Open on April 27, 2025, scoring 8/9 and achieving a 2833 performance rating. Seeded eighth, Ivanchuk outplayed younger stars like Nihal Sarin and Pranav Venkatesh, remaining unbeaten in 27 classical games over 19 days. His victory marks his first major tournament win since 2019, signaling a triumphant return to elite chess. The event also saw 11-year-old Faustino Oro nearly secure a grandmaster norm, highlighting the blend of veteran and prodigy talent in modern chess.
